Playgrounds

I treat these projects the way some people treat a workshop or a garage band. They're where I practice, break things, and follow rabbit holes with no pressure to “monetize” anything. The domains, sites, and brands below are real, but they're mostly experiments—ways to sharpen skills, test ideas in the wild, and see what actually helps real people without the usual agency gloss. If you find something useful here, great. If not, it was still time well spent because I got to make something. And if I'm tired of that, I'm grabbing my camera.

Boldspark

boldspark.co

Boldspark is my long-running lab for local SEO and simple, honest service websites in North Texas. I'm experimenting with writing in plain English, tracking what actually moves the needle in search, and keeping everything light enough that a real person can maintain it. Along the way I've learned a lot about how small businesses show up (or don’t) on Google and why clear, non-hypey copy tends to win.

Outwit

outwit.digital

Outwit is where I explore the “strategy” side of marketing without the buzzwords. I use it to test ways of diagnosing what’s really broken in a marketing system, then document experiments, case studies, and notes in public. It’s less a company and more a notebook for my own thinking about positioning, messaging, and making decisions with limited time and budget.

Bluebonnet Growth

bluebonnetgrowth.com

Bluebonnet Growth is my hyperlocal sandbox for Melissa and North Texas—focused on maps, local search, and how neighborhood businesses actually get discovered. Here I tinker with Google Business Profiles, local content, and small tweaks that compound over time. The fun part is seeing how quiet, consistent changes can shift how a town finds the people who serve it.
